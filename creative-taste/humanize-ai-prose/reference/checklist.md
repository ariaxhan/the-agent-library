# Humanize: the taste checklist

Run after the seven transform passes in `SKILL.md`. These are judgment calls; no
find-and-replace can make them. Answer each honestly. A "no" means the piece goes back in.

## Identity

- [ ] **Could only this author have written it?** Swap the byline. Is there at least one
  detail, opinion, or aside that wouldn't survive the swap?
- [ ] **Is there one detail only the author could know?** Not "a memorable moment" but the
  actual thing that happened, with a name on it. If it's missing and you can't supply it,
  the bracket placeholder stays in and the piece isn't done.
- [ ] **Does the piece take a side?** A real position someone could disagree with, stated
  without hedging. Perfect argumentative balance is an AI tell.

## Texture

- [ ] **Would you say this out loud?** Pick the three most polished sentences and say them.
  If any reads like a press release, rewrite it in spoken words.
- [ ] **Did the mic drops earn it?** Max two one-line dramatic closers, each landing on the
  piece's actual strongest beat, not just a section boundary. A mic drop on a
  medium-strength point is worse than no mic drop.
- [ ] **Is the looseness real or performed?** A planted "lol" or strategically casual
  fragment reads worse than clean prose. Looseness should come from drafting fast, not from
  decorating slow prose with casual markers.

## Honesty

- [ ] **Is every admitted flaw true?** The friction pass requires real limits; invented
  friction is worse than none. Only state limits that actually exist.
- [ ] **Honest, not gotcha.** No dunking, no debunk framing, no strawmanned opposition.
  Say what was learned and what it means.
- [ ] **Are the numbers and names verifiable?** Every specific added in Pass 1 must be
  real. A fake-specific detail is the one failure mode this system can't recover from.

## Register dial (genre judgment)

The passes are constant; how far you turn the dial isn't.

| Surface | Dial |
|---|---|
| Article / newsletter / blog | Full voice: asides, opinions, friction, first person everywhere |
| Docs / READMEs | Plain and warm, contractions yes, mic drops no, friction only where it's load-bearing ("this breaks if...") |
| Application / formal statement | Specifics and friction carry it; slang off; stay concrete |
| Email | Shortest register: one specific, one ask, no performance |

## Final question

- [ ] **Would a tired editor, reading this at 11pm, feel a person behind it?** Not "is it
  correct," not "is it polished." A person. If you hesitate, find the deadest paragraph and
  put something true in it.

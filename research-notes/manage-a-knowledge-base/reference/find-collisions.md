# Find Collisions

Surface where notes secretly disagree, independently reinforce each other, or bridge across domains nobody connected.

## Steps

1. **Scope it** — a topic, a date range, or the whole base.
2. **Read across** the note bodies and metadata, looking for three collision types:
   - **Contradiction** — two notes make claims that can't both be true.
   - **Reinforcement** — independent notes converge on the same claim from different angles.
   - **Unexpected bridge** — a structural similarity links two notes from unrelated domains.
3. **Record each collision** with its type, the notes involved, and a one-sentence claim-diff.
4. **Apply a confidence threshold** — surface only strong collisions.
5. **Route the strong ones:** flag high-confidence contradictions inline on the source notes as warnings; seed high-confidence unexpected bridges into the idea backlog as new kernels.

## Non-obvious know-how

- **One strong collision beats ten maybes.** False positives erode trust in the whole exercise — if the collision finder cries wolf, people stop reading it. Bias toward precision over recall.
- A reinforcement across *independent* notes is evidence; the same point restated in your own words twice is not.

## Output

A dated collisions log (type / notes / claim-diff per entry) plus inline annotations on the conflicting source notes.

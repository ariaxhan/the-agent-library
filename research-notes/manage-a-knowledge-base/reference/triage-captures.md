# Triage Captures

Turn a pile of raw captures — clippings, voice notes, snippets, half-thoughts — into filed, structured notes without drowning in them.

## Steps

1. **List everything** in the inbox with its capture status. Don't act yet — see the whole pile first.
2. **Infer type and topic** for each from where it came and its first couple hundred characters. Tag it provisionally.
3. **Present in small batches** — about five at a time — each with proposed type, topic, and destination, for an accept / edit / skip decision.
4. **On accept:** rewrite the capture with real structure (a title, the claim, why it matters) and move it to its canonical home. Mark it active.
5. **On skip:** leave it in the inbox with a one-line reason, so the next pass knows why it stalled.
6. **Log the counts** processed / filed / skipped.

## Non-obvious know-how

- **Batch the human-judgment moment — never auto-promote.** The inbox *is* the moment you decide what's worth keeping. The assistant proposes; the human accepts. Skipping that step turns the system into noise.
- **Cadence is the real health metric.** A five-minute daily pass keeps the pile bounded. A weekly pass is the fallback. If you're triaging monthly, the inbox has already failed — the backlog is unbounded.

## Output

Filed, structured notes in their canonical homes; a skip queue with reasons; a per-session counts log.

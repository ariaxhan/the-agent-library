# Synthesize Notes

Knit several existing notes into one integrated piece aimed at a question: the synthesis tier above single-note capture.

## Steps

1. **Pick the scope**: a tag, a project, or an explicit list. Require at least three sources.
2. **Read the inputs** fully before drafting.
3. **Draft the synthesis** with four parts:
   - **Thesis**: the integrated claim.
   - **Support**: what the sources establish.
   - **Dissent**: where they disagree or where the thesis is weak. This section is mandatory; a synthesis without dissent is propaganda.
   - **Open questions**: what's still unresolved.
4. **Write it as a new note**, distinct from its sources.
5. **Backlink every input** so the synthesis and its sources point at each other.
6. If a strong, durable claim emerges, promote it to the long-term learnings file.

## Non-obvious know-how

- **Fewer than three sources is just a longer summary.** Synthesis needs enough material to find tension between.
- **Weave toward a question, never for novelty.** A synthesis with no question it's answering is a pile of connections nobody needs.

## Output

One synthesis note integrating three or more sources (thesis / support / dissent / open questions), with bidirectional backlinks to every input.

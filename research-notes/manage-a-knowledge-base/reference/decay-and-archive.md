# Decay and Archive

Keep the knowledge base lean by letting cold notes fall away and proven ones rise — a forgetting curve for your second brain. Run it periodically (e.g. weekly).

## Steps

1. **Score every note** by use and age — roughly: how often it's been accessed, weighted by recency, divided by how old it is.
2. **Bucket by score:**
   - Low score and old → archive.
   - High score → promote up a maturity tier (seed → sapling → evergreen).
3. **Rewrite the live collection** with the survivors; move archived notes aside (don't delete).
4. **Log the full scorecard** so the decisions are auditable.
5. **Print a summary** — archived / promoted / surviving counts.

## Non-obvious know-how

- **Evergreen is never auto-archived.** Once a note is marked durable, age and access count can't retire it — protect it explicitly, or the formula will eventually bury your most important reference.
- Decay is about attention, not deletion. The archive is recoverable; the point is to keep the working set small enough to actually use.

## Output

Archive entries for cold notes, tier promotions for proven ones, a scorecard log of every decision, and an archived / promoted / surviving summary line.

# LD, Policy, World Schools, Big Questions, Extemp Debate

## Lincoln-Douglas

Use when the clash is values-first.

Ask:
- What value is being protected?
- What criterion decides that value?
- Which side better satisfies the criterion?

Good for autonomy vs safety, speed vs truth, privacy vs utility, friction vs protection.

## Policy Debate

Use for technical plans.

Aff:
- plan
- solvency
- advantages

Neg:
- disadvantage
- counterplan
- solvency attack
- topicality/scope challenge when the plan exceeds the ask

Good for architecture, infra, security, memory systems, and high-blast-radius changes.

## World Schools

Use for team strategy and clean synthesis.

Roles:
- speaker 1: framing and case
- speaker 2: extension and clash
- speaker 3: collapse and weighing
- reply: biased judge explaining why the team wins

Allow short points of information: one-sentence interruptions that test the case.

## Big Questions

Use for foundational questions where the answer changes future doctrine.

Force definitions first. A bad definition poisons the whole round.

## Extemporaneous Debate

Use when time is short but clash still matters.

Pattern:
- 5-10 minute research sprint
- pro case
- con case
- short crossfire
- decision

Good for fast "is this worth doing now?" calls.

# Limited Preparation

## Impromptu

Use when the user gives a messy idea, a fragment, or a sudden question and needs usable shape now.

Rules:
- choose one frame fast
- use 2-3 balanced points
- make the close do real work
- do not over-research

Agent adaptation:
1. Restate the prompt as a tension or image.
2. Pick a frame: contrast, ladder, before/after, three lenses, or story-to-principle.
3. Draft the answer in short spoken paragraphs.

## Extemporaneous Speaking

Use when the answer needs current facts or source-grounded analysis.

Classic shape:
- limited prep
- direct answer to a question
- 2-3 areas of analysis
- sourced support
- clear significance

Agent adaptation:
1. Ask the question exactly.
2. Give the answer early.
3. Support with live/current sources when facts may have changed.
4. End with implication, not summary mush.

Use for morning briefs, trend calls, incident reads, and "what should we think about X?".
